Setup

Up and running in 3 minutes

No technical knowledge needed. If you can open a Mac app, you can use BB Tracker.

1

Download and open

Download the .dmg and drag BB Tracker into your Applications folder.

First launch — important: macOS will show one of these messages:

  • "BB Tracker can't be opened because Apple cannot check it for malicious software"
  • or, on macOS Sequoia: "BB Tracker is damaged and can't be opened"

This is normal — the app isn't notarized yet (we're working on it). To open it:

  1. In Applications, right-click (or Control-click) BB Tracker → Open.
  2. Click Open in the dialog that appears.
  3. If that doesn't work (Sequoia 15+): open System Settings → Privacy & Security, scroll to the bottom, and click Open Anyway next to the BB Tracker line.

One-time only. After this, BB Tracker — including future updates — opens silently. More on why this happens →

2

Log in to Blackboard

A browser window opens. Sign in with your IE account and approve the Authenticator prompt. The browser closes itself.

3

Done — it's in your menu bar

BB Tracker reads your courses, syllabi, grades, and attendance automatically in the background — refresh interval is configurable in Settings. The login keeps you signed in for around 1–3 months, then you'll be prompted to log in again when Microsoft rotates the session.

Frequently asked

About BB Tracker

How the app works, where your data lives, and why macOS warns you on first launch.

How does BB Tracker get my Blackboard data?
The exact same way you do — through your own browser session.

When you first open the app, a regular Chromium window appears (built into the app, no install). You sign in to Blackboard with your IE Microsoft account, just like you would in Safari or Chrome. Your session cookie is then saved to your Mac (in ~/Library/Application Support/BBTracker). On a schedule you control in Settings (default every 2 hours), BB Tracker reuses that cookie to load your gradebook and attendance pages, parse the visible numbers, and show them in the menu bar.

That means BB Tracker can only see what you can see when you log in to Blackboard yourself — no admin access, no API keys, no special permissions.
Where does my data live? Who can see it?
Only you. BB Tracker has no server — there's no backend that your data gets sent to. Everything is stored locally on your Mac:
  • ~/Library/Application Support/BBTracker/browser_data/ — your saved Blackboard session (cookies)
  • ~/Library/Application Support/BBTracker/data.json — last scrape: course names, grades, absences
  • ~/Library/Application Support/BBTracker/courses.json — your course list and parsed syllabus weights
The only network calls BB Tracker makes go to two places: blackboard.ie.edu (to fetch your data) and api.lemonsqueezy.com (only to verify your license key, no personal data attached). That's it. No analytics, no telemetry, no ads, no third-party SDKs.
Is my Blackboard password stored anywhere?
No. The app never sees your password — you type it directly into Microsoft's login page in the browser window. What gets saved is the session cookie Microsoft hands back after authentication — the same kind your browser stores when you tick "Stay signed in." It expires every 1–3 months, and at that point you'll log in again.
Why does macOS warn me that "Apple cannot verify the developer"?
BB Tracker is currently distributed without an Apple Developer signature. To get one, the developer pays Apple $99/year and submits the app to Apple for "notarization" (an automated security scan). We're going through that process — until it's done, macOS treats the app the same way it treats anything you download from outside the App Store: with a warning on first launch.

It's not malware. It just means Apple hasn't reviewed it. The fix is one-time and takes 5 seconds:
  • Open your Applications folder.
  • Right-click BB Tracker → choose Open.
  • Click Open in the dialog that appears.
On macOS Sequoia (15+), if that doesn't work: System Settings → Privacy & Security → scroll to the bottom → click "Open Anyway".

You only need to do this once. After that the app launches normally.
Will BB Tracker affect my Blackboard account, grades, or attendance?
No. BB Tracker only reads the same pages you'd open in your browser. It doesn't post messages, submit assignments, mark attendance, or change any setting. From Blackboard's perspective it looks the same as you opening a tab to check your gradebook.
Why does the app need to log in to Blackboard?
Blackboard doesn't expose a public API for grades or attendance. The only way to read your data is to load the same web pages you'd visit yourself, and that requires being signed in. Hence the one-time login flow on first launch — after that, BB Tracker reuses the saved session for as long as Microsoft keeps it alive (typically 1–3 months).
What happens when my trial ends?
The background scraping pauses, but you keep the menu bar icon and your last-known data. You'll see a "Subscription expired — click to renew" prompt. Activate a license (paste the key in Settings → License) and everything resumes immediately. No reinstall, no losing your config.
Can I use BB Tracker on more than one Mac?
Yes — one license is bound to one Blackboard account, not one machine. You can move it between Macs by deactivating the key in Settings → License on the old Mac, then activating it on the new one.

Troubleshooting

Common issues & fixes

Try these first. If none of them work, click Help & Feedback in the menu — pick the closest category and the email pre-fills with whatever debug info we need.

macOS says “BB Tracker is damaged” or “can’t be opened”
We’re not yet notarized by Apple, so first launch is blocked. Fix: in your Applications folder, right-click BB Tracker → Open, then click Open in the dialog. On macOS Sequoia (15+) you may need: System Settings → Privacy & Security → scroll to the bottom → click “Open Anyway”.
The app stays on “Starting browser…” for a long time
Almost always means an old BB Tracker process is still running and holding the browser profile lock. Fix:
  1. Quit BB Tracker (menu bar icon → Quit).
  2. Open Activity Monitor, search “BB Tracker” and force-quit any leftover processes.
  3. Re-open BB Tracker.
Still stuck after 1 minute? Delete ~/Library/Application Support/BBTracker/browser_data and reopen — you’ll need to log in to Blackboard once more.
The menu shows old data even after refresh
macOS doesn’t live-update menus that are already open. Fix: click anywhere outside the menu to close it, then click the menu-bar icon again. You’ll get a notification when fresh data is ready.
It says “🔑 Session expired — click to re-login”
Microsoft rotates your IE session token every 1–3 months. This is normal. Click the message, complete the Blackboard login, tick “Yes” when Microsoft asks “Stay signed in?”, and BB Tracker will resume.
A specific course shows wrong / missing grades or attendance
Different courses use different Blackboard layouts (Spanish locale, custom attendance columns like QWAttendance, etc.) and we may not have a parser for yours yet. To report it, click Help & Feedback → Report missing grades or attendance in the menu — the debug log auto-attaches. Please also include:
  • A screenshot of the Blackboard gradebook page for that course
  • The exact course name as Blackboard shows it
  • What BB Tracker shows vs. what you expect
I bought a license but activation failed
Most common cause: no internet at the moment of activation. We need to verify the key with Lemon Squeezy once. Fix: reconnect to wifi, then Settings → License → paste key → Activate again. Still failing? Click Help & Feedback → Report a license or payment issue from the menu — your order number and the activation error help us diagnose fast.
I want to uninstall completely
Quit the app, drag BB Tracker.app from Applications to the bin, and delete ~/Library/Application Support/BBTracker. That removes the browser profile, your config, and the cached data.
